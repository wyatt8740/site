Open Letter to """"A.I."""" Companies

You are destroying the planet.

I am not even exaggerating when I say that.

Computing hardware has grown a lot in the last 50 years or so; a single chip powered by a couple of AA batteries now can do incredible things that would have required an AC mains connection before. I'd like to first applaud the efforts of all the researchers, scientists, and engineers who have gotten us to this point.

But no matter how much we grow our capabilities, we are never happy with it.

We only target increased performance; no one markets their products to consumers with the intent of keeping them mindful of power consumption. Indeed, that gets brushed under the rug, because "look at this ray-tracing!" or more recently "Look! AI!!! Buy our product!".

If the public were informed in marketing about the importance of sustainability, and humanity as a whole (INCLUDING corporate America) really were to try to advance computing and electronics in general with a goal of 'consuming less power each generation to achieve the SAME performance' instead of 'increasing "performance" – those penguins had it coming, anyway', then MAYBE we'd actually be starting to fix things, instead of accelerating our energy consumption for Somebody Else (our children and grandchildren) to deal with.

Google recently added an "AI Overview" field to its search results, which means every single search that gets fed through that is now consuming computing time on a GPU or other special silicon somewhere. Companies are now experimenting with putting datacenters in the ocean because they are paying so much in water bills to keep their servers cool, even as the public is being told to ration water in places like California that are experiencing drought and wildfires. Nvidia is launching GPU's for datacenters that draw in excess of one kilowatt which they anticipate they will sell 3,500,000 of.

By the way, "carbon offsets" are smoke and mirrors, with little evidence to support the idea that paying them actually makes a company carbon neutral.

It's not just hardware engineers and engineering companies or IT firms that should be ashamed; programmers should, too. We keep demanding more and more, using more CPU cycles to perform the same tasks we were doing in the 1990's, but rewritten in some higher level language to keep employed.

If we were still content to play games that looked like Quake, or even like The Legend of Zelda: Twilight Princess or Portal 2, and if we were still content to have static webpages, or server-rendered pages that didn't automatically update without user interaction via REST API's and Javascript, then I suspect that even though the population has grown and there are now a billion more people on the planet than when I was in school, our energy consumption might not be the unmitigated disaster it is now. We've grown complacent (or maybe we always were?), and have decided that we can just tell the user to buy a more powerful computer (and produce more e-waste).

The takeaway that I'm trying to get at here is this:

Increasing performance per watt will not save the planet unless you don't also increase your expectations of performance proportionately.

We need to stop looking the other way and dismissing this as "someone else's problem" because every single time we do a google search and see an "AI overview," we should not think huh, that's neat. We should be angry. Because these companies have once again shown us that they do not actually care about anything but perpetual growth for stockholders. They do not have a responsible bone in their bodies with the weight and power to actually make the changes that need to be made.

You, yes, you (insert A.I. company here), are killing the planet, all while you pat yourself on the back for destroying the careers of artists and programmers alike by replacing them.

And you should be ashamed.

And if you care at all, you should lower your expectations of computers.

—Wyatt
