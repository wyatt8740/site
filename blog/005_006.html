Unicomp Returns to Form:
Thoughts on the Unicomp Mini M Keyboard

14 June 2021 [Permanent link]

The Unicomp Mini M is, to my knowledge, the first entirely new keyboard design Unicomp has produced since it started in around 1996 (the year that I was born). This is my own unit, which has already been subject to a couple small alterations (note the bottom key row).

Unicomp, based in Lexington, Kentucky, is a company with an unusual history. Its continued ability to exist is honestly something of a miracle to me, although I'm still quite happy that it seems to manage. They are the sole company that has been a source for IBM membrane-buckling-spring keyboards (and parts for them) for the past twenty-five years or so.

They appear to have started when Lexmark spun off its keyboard manufacturing arm (Lexmark itself having been spun off of IBM a few years earlier). For most of their existance, they have mainly acted as a company that manufactures keyboards for a variety of IBM terminals and other IBM/Lexmark equipment.

Over the last quarter century, however, many have observed that the company hasn't responded much to demand changes or indeed shown many attempts at expanding their business. Indeed, over the years, they have discontinued production of a fair number of their products (for instance, their Mighty Mouse keyboard with trackpoint, which was modelled after a thinkpad keyboard).
